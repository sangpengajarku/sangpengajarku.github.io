Descriptive text adalah suatu teks yang menjelaskan atau mendeskripsikan orang orang, hewan, tempat atau suatu benda baik bentuknya, sifat-sifatnya, jumlahnya dan lain-lain.
Teks deskripsi sering kali menggunakan kata be dan have, yang kerap digunakan dalam bentuk simple present tense. Namun, bila hal yang akan anda jelaskan sudah tidak lagi ada, anda bisa menggunakan past tense untuk menggambarkannya.

Deskripsi PantaiPembahasan

Seaside

a Seaside or the beach is a geographical form consisting of sand, and is found in coastal areas of the sea. The coastal area is the boundary between land and sea waters. The length of the coastline is measured around the entire coast which is a territorial area of a country.

According to the 2008 UN correction, Indonesia is the fourth longest coastline country in the world after the United States (US), Canada and Russia. The length of the Indonesian coastline is recorded at 95,181 km.

Coastline

The coastline is the boundary between the sea and land in the highest tide sea level. The sea line can change due to abrasion, which is the erosion of the coast by the impact of ocean waves which causes a reduction in land area.

There are several important steps that can be taken to secure the coastline such as breakwater and the development of vegetation on the coast.

To overcome the abrasion / scouring of the shoreline from waves / waves can be used a breakwater that serves to reflect back wave energy. Various ways are taken to break the waves including using a stack of tetrapods made of concrete at a certain distance from the coastline.

Mangrove forests can help overcome waves and at the same time benefit animal life and breeding grounds for certain fish. Mangroves in most of the North coast have been lost due to human activity, which in turn will erode the coast.

Coral reefs are also natural breakwaters, so it is very necessary to be conserved and developed in maintaining coastlines.

A mosquito is a type of fly. It is the common name of a family of flies in the order Diptera.

The females are ectoparasites: they land on warm-blooded animals, pierce a capillary, and inject saliva to stop the blood coagulating. Then they suck up and eat the blood. Deadly microscopic parasites often live in the saliva.

The males are nectar-feeders, and so are the females. However, in preparation for egg-laying the females turn to blood for its protein.
